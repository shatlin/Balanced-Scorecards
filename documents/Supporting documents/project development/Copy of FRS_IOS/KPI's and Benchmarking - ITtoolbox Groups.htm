KPI's and Benchmarking

Asked by Paul on 2/19/2004 5:09:00 PM
I am working with a number of Australian based SME's (<$A200M) in the manufacturing sector who are begining to explore electronic procurement between existing business partners within existing supply chains. The business driver may be twofold; businesses that see eCommerce as a market differentiator and/or have supply chain partners wanting to engage in eCommerce and wanting to coerce other partners to use electronic functionality or leave the supply chain.

The project is looking to explore software solutions (such as messaging and P2P) to provide base level functionality for the procure-to-pay business cycle between established business partners using their existing ERP's (tier 2,3 and 4)at minimal cost.

To ensure focussed meetings and project outcomes I am looking to provide the firms with some sample KPI's and ideally, sectoral benchmarks for the procure-to-pay cycle. This is to ensure that when meeting with these firms, I can quickly document their requisite business outcome/issues and the relevant KPI's. In my experience, many SME's have little idea about KPI's and I thought it better not to start with a "blank board" when meeting with clients to avoid any embarrassment.

I have compiled a list of sample KPI's and I would appreciate any comments from the list.

1.The reduction of at least 70% in the cost of errors caused by document re-keying; 2.The reduction of at least 90% in the cost of errors through data validation during document generation; 3.The reduction of order processing time to at least 50% of the current level; 4.Labour savings or redeployment from the elimination of rekeying messages; 5.The efficient production and distribution of dynamic electronic price lists; 6.The automatic production and distribution of multiple export documentation; 7.The ability to lodge electronic versions of documents with multiple parties; 8.Ability to make data available electronically with minimal resource requirement or without purchase of a new ERP; 9.Delivery of automatic validation and resolution of data values such as part numbers and units of measure between trading partners; 10.Increase productivity and staff morale by redeployment of skills. 11. Minimal disruptive impact to existing IT infrastructure; 12. Minimise current reduction of paper documents by 50%; 13.Enable secure direct communication of electronic business documents between customers/ and or suppliers.

Additionally, I am looking for internationally accepted benchmarks for the procure-to-pay process for SME's in the manufacturing sector.

Any comment or direction to references from the list would be gratefully accepted. My apologies if I have posted to the wrong list.

Paul
